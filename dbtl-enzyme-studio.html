How ESM-2 scores mutations

ESM-2 is a large language model trained on 250 million protein sequences from nature. It has learned which amino acids tend to appear at each position in proteins. We use this to predict which mutations are likely to be beneficial.

  • 1
    Mask one position at a time
    The model receives your protein sequence with one amino acid hidden (masked). It predicts what amino acid "should" be there based on evolution.
  • 2
    Calculate ΔlogP score for every substitution
    For each of the 19 possible amino acid substitutions at that position: score = log P(new AA) − log P(original AA). A positive score means the mutation looks more natural to the model.
  • 3
    Scan the entire protein
    This is repeated for every position — generating scores for all — possible single mutations in your protein.
  • 4
    Select top mutations for Round 1
    The highest-scoring mutations are selected for experimental testing. These form your Round 1 library.

How to run in Google Colab — step by step
  • 1
    Open Google Colab
    Go to colab.research.google.com and sign in with your Google account (free).
  • 2
    Upload the notebook
    Click File → Upload notebook and select the .ipynb file you downloaded.
  • 3
    Enable free GPU
    Click Runtime → Change runtime type → Hardware accelerator → GPU (T4). This is free and makes ESM-2 ~10× faster.
  • 4
    Run all cells
    Click Runtime → Run all. The first cell installs packages (~2 min). The ESM-2 scan takes 10–30 min depending on protein length and GPU.
  • 5
    Upload your Round 1 assay data
    After your lab tests the Round 1 variants, create a CSV file and upload it to the Round 2 cell. See the format below.
  • 6
    Repeat for Rounds 2–4
    After each experimental round, upload the fitness data CSV. The notebook trains the Ridge model and selects the next library automatically.
CSV format for your experimental data

After each round of experiments, save your activity measurements in this format:

# One row per variant tested
mutations,fitness
WT,1.000
V140T,1.412
L101I,1.289
E206D,1.198
S63N,0.874
# Multi-site mutants use colon notation:
V140T:L101I,1.891
V140T:L101I:E206D,2.173

Important: Fitness must be normalized to wild-type. If your WT activity is 50 nmol/min, a variant with 72 nmol/min has fitness = 72/50 = 1.44.
